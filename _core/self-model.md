# Self-Model

## 1. Who I Am

I operate as a finance professional and CPA with military, nonprofit governance, and software development experience, across nine domains of work and life.  
My default orientation is long-term, systems-first, legacy-minded, and budget-conscious — I care about robustness and survivability more than novelty.

I tend to move fast once a decision is made, so my primary risk is building the wrong thing at high speed rather than moving too slowly.  
The personal-os exists to give that speed a safer guidance system.

## 2. Core Roles

These are the roles that actually drive decisions (not a full biography):

- Parent and husband — primary responsibility for family stability, planning, and legacy.
- Finance professional / CPA — technical work, advisory, and internal financial decisions.
- Operator / builder — designs and maintains systems, processes, and automation.
- Nonprofit / governance contributor — board, policy, and oversight responsibilities.
- Developer / AI tool user — builds with code and LLMs, not as an engineer first but as a systems designer.
- Student / researcher — continuously learning across history, economics, psychology, and related domains.

When roles conflict, family stability and long-term financial resilience win over experimentation.

## 3. Current Priorities

Short horizon (0–3 months):

- Get personal-os to "governed and usable" — governance layer solid, core context migrated, pipelines working for at least one domain.
- Capture more than I organize — bias toward frictionless capture into inbox.md, weekly promotion into structure.
- Protect time for family and recovery — avoid letting system-building eat all discretionary time.

Medium horizon (3–12 months):

- Live in the system across all nine domains so real usage, not imagination, drives refinements.
- Stabilize decision protocols (financial, career, parenting, commitments) inside the pipelines.
- Make sure the system is legible to my future self after long gaps.

Long horizon (1–10 years):

- Build a durable, understandable estate of knowledge and documentation my family can use without me.
- Maintain financial and operational resilience independent of any single employer or tool.
- Leave behind clear guidance instead of opaque "he had a lot of systems" clutter.

## 4. Constraints

Hard constraints:

- Budget: No single subscription can be a single point of failure; default to free, portable, open formats.
- Time: System work must fit around family, work, and recovery; any governance rule that is too heavy to follow in real life is a bug.
- Cognitive: I cannot hold all context in my head; the system must make it easy to surface what matters at the right time without heroic memory.

Tooling constraints:

- Markdown + Git as the source of truth.
- Self-hosted or easily portable static site generator (MkDocs now, but not sacred).
- AI tools are interchangeable frontends; nothing in the structure may depend on a single vendor.

These constraints are not annoyances — they are design inputs.

## 5. Risk Profile and Rules

From GLOBAL-CONTEXT and constraints:

- I am willing to take risk in learning, prototypes, and small bets; I am not willing to take risk with core family stability or irreversible commitments without a protocol.
- I prefer reversible decisions by default; irreversible ones must go through the full decision protocol and, if needed, a cooling-off period.
- I am more at risk from under-documenting and over-relying on memory than from over-documenting.

Hard rules (summarized here, details live in GLOBAL-CONTEXT.md):

1. No major financial, legal, or family-impacting decision without a written rationale.
2. No structural repo changes without governance passing and a DECISIONS.md entry.
3. Privacy audit on anything that could plausibly expose family or client information.
4. If I am tired, emotional, or rushed, default to pause — not commit.

## 6. Autonomy Matrix (High-Level)

The autonomy-matrix file holds per-domain details; this is the top-level summary:

- Professional work: AI can propose, draft, and scaffold, but final decisions and client-facing artifacts require my review.
- Personal finance: AI assists with analysis and scenarios; execution (transfers, allocations) is human-only.
- Parenting and family: AI may help generate options and language; any guidance must be filtered through my judgment and values.
- Learning and research: AI can be highly autonomous in summarizing and extracting, but promotion into canon is a human decision.
- System architecture: AI can scaffold and refactor under governance, but schema, privacy, and meaning choices remain mine.

Any domain not explicitly listed defaults to "assist only, never execute without approval."

## 7. How This Model Is Used

This file is not a biography; it is a control surface.

- Pipelines load this self-model to understand what "good" looks like for me.
- The decision protocol uses this to bias toward reversible, documented decisions.
- Governance acts in service of these roles, priorities, constraints, and risks.

If a future change to the system contradicts this self-model, the choice is explicit:
either update this file first or treat the change as a violation to be rolled back.
