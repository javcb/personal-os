# Family & Parenting

Domain for family stability, parenting decisions, relationship dynamics, and personal legacy.

---

## Purpose & Scope

**Why this domain matters:**

Parent and husband is the primary role. Family stability, children's wellbeing, and long-term legacy matter more than any professional achievement or system-building. This domain covers parenting approaches, family decision-making, relationship health, and cultural/values transmission to the next generation. It is values-driven and context-dependent; one-size-fits-all parenting advice is often wrong for your specific family.

**What is in scope:**
- Parenting philosophy and approaches
- Family planning and major family decisions (education, moves, commitments)
- Relationship and household dynamics
- Children's development and milestones
- Grief, conflict, and family transitions
- Legacy and values transmission
- Family financial planning (household budgets, college planning, inheritance)
- Cultural and spiritual practices within the family

**What is explicitly out of scope:**
- Professional parenting therapy or clinical intervention (work with professionals directly)
- Other families' parenting choices (focus on own family)
- General parenting theory without family context
- Professional childcare operations (if that ever becomes relevant)

## Key Models & Frameworks

**Mental models you apply in this domain:**

- **Systems thinking for family**: Family is a system with feedback loops, trade-offs, and unintended consequences. Optimizing one subsystem (e.g., financial) can degrade another (e.g., relationship time). Decisions must account for system health, not just individual goals.
- **Long-term parenting**: Decisions made now shape children's behavior, values, and relationship patterns for decades. Short-term convenience often has long-term costs. Reverse-engineer from desired adult outcomes.
- **Family stability as prerequisite**: Safe, stable family environment is prerequisite for all other work. Protect it fiercely. Any system-building, professional growth, or experimentation must fit within family first, not the reverse.
- **Values as decision filter**: Family decisions should align with long-term values and legacy you want to leave. When unsure, choose options that model the values you want children to internalize.
- **Reversibility in parenting**: Many parenting decisions are irreversible (moving for school, starting homeschool, major discipline choices). Irreversible parenting decisions require deliberation, consultation, and alignment with spouse.

**Key concepts:**
- **Family culture**: Explicitly cultivated norms around meals, conflict resolution, learning, fun, and resilience. Culture is designed, not defaulted.
- **Presence over productivity**: Time with family is primary work. System-building is servant to family time, not competitor.
- **Seasons and phases**: Parenting needs change with children's ages and family circumstances. Strategies that work in one season may not in another.

## Operating Rules & Decision Protocols

**Rules that govern work in this domain:**

1. **Family stability wins over everything else** — If a decision (professional opportunity, system project, financial choice) threatens family stability or relationship health, the decision is no. Non-negotiable.

2. **Major family decisions require spouse alignment and deliberation** — Anything affecting family structure, children's education/safety, or major commitments is a joint decision with cooling-off time and multiple conversations.

3. **Parenting approaches are values-based, not dogmatic** — Other families' successful strategies may not work for yours. Test approaches, get feedback from spouse and (age-appropriate) children, adjust based on your family's actual culture and values.

4. **No decision when tired, emotional, or rushed** — Parenting moments happen under stress. Default to pause/delay, not immediate action. Cool off first.

5. **Time protection is sacred** — System-building, professional work, and learning must fit around family time, not the reverse. If "governing personal-os" eats family time, the system is broken.

**Decision protocol (if applicable):**

If a major family decision or parenting question arises:
1. Pause. Don't decide under stress or fatigue.
2. Consult spouse — this is a joint decision if it affects family structure or children.
3. Identify the values at stake — what kind of family culture or adult outcomes do you want?
4. Brainstorm options with spouse; research if needed (but remember: your family context matters more than parenting dogma).
5. Trial or soft-launch if possible — test approach with children; gather feedback.
6. Document the decision and rationale in _core/ so you can revisit and learn later.
7. Debrief after a month or season — is it working? Adjust.

## Autonomy Boundaries

**Reference from autonomy-matrix.md:**

| Action Type | Autonomy | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Analysis | AI → Review | AI can help analyze parenting concepts or family dynamics; Javier's values and family context must validate |
| Drafting/Proposals | AI → Review | AI can draft communication language, options, or scenario analysis; Javier and spouse review and adjust |
| Execution | Human | All parenting choices, discipline, and family agreements are made by Javier and spouse |
| Final Decision | Human | Family decisions are Javier's and spouse's joint decision |

**What this means in practice:**

LLMs can help analyze parenting questions (child development concepts, communication strategies, conflict approaches) but must defer to Javier's judgment about what fits the family. LLMs can draft language for conversations or option analyses. But parenting decisions and discipline are Javier and spouse's domain — LLMs don't execute or override.
