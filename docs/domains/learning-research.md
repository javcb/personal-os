# Learning & Research

Domain for learning, research, intellectual curiosity, and knowledge synthesis across domains.

---

## Purpose & Scope

**Why this domain matters:**

As a student and researcher, continuous learning drives intellectual growth and feeds all other domains. This domain covers intentional learning in history, economics, psychology, systems thinking, and adjacent fields. It's where ideas are explored, frameworks are tested, and knowledge is synthesized before integration into personal canon.

**What is in scope:**
- Reading and intellectual exploration
- Research projects and learning tracks
- Knowledge synthesis and frameworks
- Interdisciplinary learning (history, economics, psychology, systems thinking)
- Writing and intellectual expression
- Learning management and curriculum design
- Question exploration and hypothesis testing
- Knowledge-extraction from external sources

**What is explicitly out of scope:**
- Professional learning and development (see professional-advisory)
- Domain-specific learning that belongs in other domain files
- Technical implementation of learning systems (see software-development)
- Learning agenda and planning (see strategy-planning)

## Key Models & Frameworks

**Mental models you apply in this domain:**

- **Learning as integration, not accumulation**: Collecting facts is not learning. Learning is integrating new information into existing mental models, refining models, and increasing predictive power. Does this new idea change how I think?
- **Question-driven learning**: Start with genuine questions, not topics. A good question pulls in research; a topic doesn't. "Why do financial crises happen?" is better learning than "read about economics."
- **Synthesis before promotion**: Not all learning belongs in canon. Test frameworks against experience first. Promote to docs/domains/ only after validation.
- **Reversibility in intellectual positions**: Be willing to change your mind when evidence suggests it. Intellectual humility is strength, not weakness. Document reasoning so you can revisit decisions later.
- **Teaching as learning**: The best way to deepen understanding is to teach or write about it. If you can't explain it clearly, you don't understand it yet.

**Key concepts:**
- **Known unknowns vs. unknown unknowns**: Knowing what you don't know is progress. _core/quadrants.md tracks these. Learning is often about moving questions from unknown unknowns to known unknowns.
- **Breadth and depth**: Breadth of reading prevents narrow thinking; depth in specific areas prevents surface-level knowledge. Balance both.
- **Learning velocity**: Some learning is fast (skimming an article); some is slow (deeply studying a book). Different questions require different velocities.

## Operating Rules & Decision Protocols

**Rules that govern work in this domain:**

1. **Learning is proposal-based, not consumption-based** — Read and research to answer questions, not to accumulate information. Questions drive reading; reading feeds questions.

2. **Synthesis happens before promotion** — Ideas stay in inbox.md or docs/knowledge-extracts/ until tested and synthesized. Only promote to docs/domains/ after validation and integration.

3. **Learning captures go to inbox.md first** — Unprocessed learning notes go to inbox with tags ([model], [quadrant], [session:date]). Weekly review decides permanent home.

4. **Knowledge extraction is structured** — External content is extracted using the knowledge-extract template (docs/templates/knowledge-extract.md) with source attribution and synthesis.

5. **Intellectual humility is required** — Be willing to revisit positions. Document reasoning so future-you can understand why you believed something. Be ready to change.

**Decision protocol (if applicable):**

If exploring a significant learning question:
1. Clarify the question: What do I really want to know? Why does this matter?
2. Check what I already know: Review _core/models.md and docs/domains/ for existing frameworks.
3. Identify sources: Where is the best thinking on this question? Books, research, people?
4. Deep learning: Read/study with the question in mind. Take notes. Look for patterns.
5. Synthesis: How does this change my thinking? What new mental model emerges?
6. Capture: Write up the synthesis and file it in inbox.md (with tags) or directly in docs/knowledge-extracts/.
7. Promote or defer: After a season, decide whether this belongs in canon (docs/domains/) or stays in extracts.

**What this means in practice:**

LLMs have high autonomy in this domain. They can research questions, analyze frameworks, draft summaries, and create knowledge-extract documents. Execution is autonomous — creating research docs doesn't require approval. But promotion to canon (docs/domains/) is Javier's decision. LLM is research assistant; Javier is knowledge architect.

## Pipelines & Knowledge Ingestion

**Pipelines that feed this domain:**

- **Research and Learning Pipeline** → Ingests research articles, books, learning materials; creates knowledge-extracts in docs/knowledge-extracts/learning-research/
- **Framework Synthesis Pipeline** → Analyzes learning output; proposes new mental models for docs/domains/learning-research/ or _core/models.md

**Knowledge sources:**

- Books, academic research, and intellectual writing
- Online courses and educational platforms
- Podcasts and interviews with domain experts
- Peer learning and teaching others
- Personal experience and reflection

**Integration triggers:**

- When a learning question surfaces: Log in _core/quadrants.md; begin research via internal-first-research.
- After deep reading or research: Create knowledge-extract with synthesis.
- Quarterly: Review learning progress; identify next questions.
- When learning is ready for promotion: Move from knowledge-extract to docs/domains/ or _core/models.md.
