# Legal & Regulatory

Domain for legal compliance, regulatory requirements, contracts, and legal risk management.

---

## Purpose & Scope

**Why this domain matters:**

Legal and regulatory compliance carry real consequences: penalties, liability, loss of licensure, or personal liability. This domain covers personal legal matters, professional compliance, regulatory obligations, and legal risk management. Legal decisions should never be made casually or without professional advice. This domain is about understanding requirements and managing risk, not providing legal advice.

**What is in scope:**
- Personal legal matters (contracts, agreements, estate planning)
- Professional licensing and regulatory compliance
- Tax compliance and reporting obligations
- Family legal matters (guardianship, adoption, custody if relevant)
- Business and nonprofit legal structures
- Privacy and data protection
- Insurance and liability management
- Legal risk identification and management

**What is explicitly out of scope:**
- Detailed legal advice (consult legal professionals)
- Litigation or dispute resolution (work with attorneys)
- Business law implementation (work with attorneys)
- Regulatory interpretation beyond basic understanding

---

## Key Models & Frameworks

**Mental models you apply in this domain:**

- **Legal compliance is non-negotiable**: Unlike many decisions, legal compliance isn't a trade-off. Failure to comply can have severe consequences. Default is to comply, not to find loopholes.
- **Professional advice is mandatory**: This domain is too high-stakes for DIY solutions. Tax questions, contract review, legal interpretation: work with qualified professionals (CPAs, attorneys).
- **Preventive law beats reactive**: Preventing legal problems (good record-keeping, clear agreements, tax planning) is cheaper and easier than fixing them later.
- **Documentation is protection**: Good documentation protects you (shows intent, proves compliance) and protects others (clear agreements prevent disputes). Document everything.
- **Regulatory change is constant**: Laws, regulations, and tax rules change. Staying current is ongoing, not one-time.

**Key concepts:**
- **Personal liability**: Understand what you're personally liable for. Insurance, business structures, and legal agreements can limit exposure.
- **Privacy and data protection**: Treat privacy as foundational. Any system that could expose family, client, or personal information requires privacy audit.
- **Statute of limitations and deadlines**: Many legal matters have strict deadlines. Missing a deadline can waive rights permanently. Create calendar reminders for deadlines.

## Operating Rules & Decision Protocols

**Rules that govern work in this domain:**

1. **No major legal decision without professional counsel** — Personal legal matters, tax strategy, compliance questions, and contract review all get professional review. CPA for tax; attorney for legal.

2. **Privacy audit on anything sensitive** — Any system or process that touches personal, family, client, or business data requires explicit privacy review. No exceptions.

3. **Regulatory changes are tracked** — Tax law, professional regulations, and relevant laws are monitored. Subscribe to alerts; schedule quarterly review.

4. **Documentation is complete** — All agreements, tax decisions, and important commitments are documented. This is proof of intent and compliance.

5. **Deadlines are in calendar** — Tax deadlines, license renewal dates, regulatory filing deadlines: all in calendar with reminders. Missing deadlines is not acceptable.

**Decision protocol (if applicable):**

If facing a legal or regulatory question:
1. Identify the legal/regulatory area: tax, contracts, licensing, estate planning, privacy, other.
2. Consult professional: CPA for tax; attorney for legal; regulatory agency for compliance.
3. Understand the requirement: What does the law/regulation require? What are the consequences of non-compliance?
4. Plan and document: Write down the plan, decision, and reasoning. Keep documentation.
5. Implement with checks: Execute the plan, then verify compliance (e.g., file the return, confirm the license renewal).
6. Monitor for changes: Regulatory landscape changes. Monitor and adjust as needed.
