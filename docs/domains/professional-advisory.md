# Professional Work & Advisory

Domain for professional work, client advisory, consulting, and professional expertise application.

---

## Purpose & Scope

**Why this domain matters:**

As a finance professional and CPA, professional work is primary income and expertise. This domain covers client advisory, consulting engagements, professional writing, and application of technical expertise. Professional reputation and ethical standards are foundational. This work must be of highest quality and must be compliant with professional standards.

**What is in scope:**
- Client advisory and consulting
- Technical work and analysis for clients/employers
- Professional writing and communication
- Ethical decision-making and standards
- Professional relationship management
- Continuing education and professional development
- Professional licensing and compliance
- Peer collaboration and mentorship

**What is explicitly out of scope:**
- Personal financial decisions (see finance-economics)
- Professional learning and research (see learning-research)
- Business strategy and operations (see strategy-planning)
- Technical implementation (see software-development)
- Regulatory compliance (see legal-regulatory)

## Key Models & Frameworks

**Mental models you apply in this domain:**

- **Expertise as trust**: Professional credibility is built on consistent delivery of quality work. One bad engagement can damage reputation built over years. Excellence is non-negotiable.
- **Ethical clarity over profit**: When professional ethics and financial incentives conflict, ethics win. This is a filter on which clients and projects to take.
- **Client context matters**: Generic best practice advice is often wrong for specific clients. Listen first; understand their constraints, values, and actual situation before proposing solutions.
- **Reversibility in professional choices**: Some professional decisions (client commitments, reputation choices, standing-up-for-ethics) are largely irreversible. Treat them with appropriate weight.
- **Continuous learning is professional obligation**: Staying current with tax law, financial regulation, and industry best practices is ongoing, not optional.

**Key concepts:**
- **Professional independence**: Maintain independence of judgment. Financial pressure or social pressure should not override professional standards.
- **Documentation and defensibility**: All significant professional advice should be documented, with clear reasoning. Defensibility is not paranoia; it's professionalism.
- **Scope clarity**: Clear scope of work, deliverables, and timelines prevent misalignment. Vague professional relationships become conflicts.

## Operating Rules & Decision Protocols

**Rules that govern work in this domain:**

1. **No professional work without scope and agreement** — Every engagement has written scope (what's included, what's not), timeline, and deliverables. Scope creep is managed explicitly.

2. **Professional quality is minimum, not aspirational** — All professional deliverables meet professional standards: accurate, documented, reviewed, compliant. This is non-negotiable, even if it extends timelines.

3. **Ethical clarity before engagement** — Before taking a client or project, clarify whether there are ethical concerns or conflicts. Walk away from engagements that conflict with professional standards or personal values.

4. **Client relationships are managed explicitly** — Difficult conversations about timelines, scope, fees, or concerns happen early and directly. Hidden concerns become larger problems.

5. **Privacy and confidentiality are absolute** — Client data, internal information, and confidential matters are protected absolutely. Privacy audit on anything that could expose client or employer information.

**Decision protocol (if applicable):**

If evaluating a professional opportunity:
1. Clarify the scope: what is the client asking for? What are the constraints and success criteria?
2. Assess ethical fit: Does this align with professional standards and personal values? Are there conflicts of interest?
3. Evaluate capacity: Do I have time and expertise? Is this a growth opportunity or distraction?
4. Define the engagement: Write scope, timeline, deliverables, fees, and success criteria.
5. Communicate expectations: Be clear about what you will and won't do, timeline, and feedback loops.
6. Execute to standard: Deliver professional work. Document reasoning. Maintain quality even if it's harder than expected.
7. Debrief: After engagement, capture learnings and document for future reference.
